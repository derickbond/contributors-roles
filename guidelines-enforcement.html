Guidelines and Enforcement

Guidelines

  1. Confidentiality:
    • The project should not be shared with anyone outside the project team without prior authorization.
    • All project-related discussions, documents, and information should be kept confidential and within the project team.
  2. Active Participation:
    • All project members are expected to actively contribute to the project's development and progress.
    • Regular engagement in discussions, meetings, and assigned tasks is required.
  3. Timely Delivery:
    • Project members should complete their assigned tasks within agreed-upon timelines.
    • Timely delivery of work ensures smooth progress and overall project success.
  4. Communication:
    • Maintain open and transparent communication within the project team.
    • Utilize project management tools, instant messaging platforms, and regular meetings to facilitate effective communication channels.
  5. Accountability:
    • Each member is accountable for their assigned tasks and responsibilities.
    • Take ownership of the work and ensure high-quality outputs.
  6. Respect and Professionalism:
    • Treat all team members with respect, professionalism, and courtesy.
    • Maintain a positive and inclusive work environment that encourages collaboration and constructive feedback.
  7. Compliance with Guidelines:
    • Adhere to project guidelines, coding standards, and best practices.
    • Follow established project processes and workflows to maintain consistency and efficiency.

Enforcement Measures

  1. Performance Assessment:
    • Regularly assess the performance and contribution of project members.
    • Identify areas of non-compliance or lack of contribution.
  2. Feedback and Support:
    • Provide feedback and constructive criticism to members who are not meeting their obligations.
    • Offer support, guidance, or additional resources to help improve performance.
  3. Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs):
    • If non-compliance or lack of contribution persists, implement a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP).
    • A PIP outlines specific expectations, timelines, and consequences if improvement is not demonstrated.
  4. Removal of Non-Contributing Members:
    • In cases of consistent non-compliance or lack of contribution, non-contributing members may be removed from the project team.
    • Removal will be done after providing sufficient opportunities and support for improvement.
